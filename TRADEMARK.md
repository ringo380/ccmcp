The **ccmcp source code** is open source under the [MIT License](LICENSE) — you
are free to use, copy, modify, and redistribute it, including as a fork or
derivative work, provided you keep the attribution required by that license
(see [NOTICE](NOTICE)).

The **"ccmcp" name and brand** are a separate matter. Open source licenses
cover *copyright*, not *names*. The MIT License grant on this project covers the
code only — it does **not** grant any right to use the "ccmcp" name, wordmark,
or logo. Those are reserved by the project author, Ryan Robson.

This policy exists so users can trust that something called "ccmcp" is the
genuine upstream project, while still leaving the code fully open to fork.

## What you may do

- **Fork, modify, and redistribute the code** under the MIT License.
- **Refer to ccmcp by name accurately** ("nominative use"): e.g. "a fork of
  ccmcp", "based on ccmcp", "compatible with ccmcp", or naming it in
  documentation, articles, and comparisons.
- **Keep upstream references** in your fork's history, license, and NOTICE.

## What you may not do without permission

- **Name your fork or derivative `ccmcp`**, or any name that incorporates or is
  confusingly similar to it — including, but not limited to, `ccmcp-better`,
  `ccmcp-plus`, `ccmcp-ng`, `ccmcp2`, `ccmcpx`, `better-ccmcp`, or `ccmcp.io`.
  Choose a clearly distinct name for your project.
- **Use the "ccmcp" name, wordmark, or logo** in a way that suggests your fork
  is the official project, is endorsed by it, or is affiliated with it.
- **Publish packages or distribution artifacts under the ccmcp name** — for
  example a Homebrew formula/tap, npm package, container image, or release
  binary named `ccmcp` — for anything other than the official upstream project.

If your use could lead a reasonable person to think your project *is* ccmcp or
is officially associated with it, it is not permitted under this policy.

## Requests

To request permission for a use not covered above, or if you have questions,
please open an issue at https://github.com/ringo380/ccmcp/issues or contact the
maintainer.
